Immigration

  Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada is the department of the government of Canada with responsibility for issues dealing with immigration, refugees, and citizenship. The department was established in 1994 following a reorganization within the federal government, and was renamed from Citizenship and Immigration Canada to its current name with the swearing in of the 29th ministry in 2015.

Step1 - Determine the eligibility

  Understanding the eligibility is to make sure who is eligible and find the program to immigrate, such as immigrant investor, Quebec-selected skilled workers, self-employed, ..., etc.

Step2 - Apply to immigrate

  Find your eligibility and apply the program that you have the eligibility.

Step3 - Prepare for life in Canada

  Finding a job, getting your credentials assessed, learning English and French and what to expect when you get to Canada, financially, socially and culturally. Different programs allow people stay ing Canada as different position, such as an employee, an employer, or a student

Some Awareness

  There are some awareness that we should know. For example, we should learn about using a representative, protect ourselves from fraud, appeal our immigration decision, learn English and/or French, get to know Canada, ..., etc.
